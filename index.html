Fengchun Qiao

I am a Ph.D. student in Computer & Information Sciences at University of Delaware, supervised by Prof. Xi Peng. My research focuses on trustworthy machine learning, with a particular emphasis on Out-of-Distribution (OOD) generalization. I develop principled optimization methods to ensure AI systems perform reliably in unseen environments—for example, enabling a flood predictor trained on historical U.S. flood events (2000–2020) to handle an unseen catastrophe like Hurricane Ida in 2021.

I will join the Bellini College of AI, Cybersecurity, and Computing at the University of South Florida as an Assistant Professor starting in Fall 2025. I am currently seeking 1–2 highly motivated PhD students to begin in Spring or Fall 2026, with full financial support. My research focuses on Trustworthy Machine Learning, AI Safety, and Foundation Models. Interested applicants are encouraged to fill out this Google form.

About USF: The University of South Florida (USF), located in Tampa, is a top-tier public research institution classified as R1 for "very high research activity" and one of only two universities in the State University System of Florida to join the prestigious Association of American Universities (AAU). In the 2025 U.S. News & World Report rankings, USF is ranked 45th among public universities and 91st overall. With over $738 million in annual research funding and a vibrant campus in the dynamic Tampa Bay area, USF offers Ph.D. students robust academic support, interdisciplinary collaboration, and access to cutting-edge facilities in a thriving metropolitan environment.
